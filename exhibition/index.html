Fading Bytes: The Ephemeral Nature of Net Art

Curated by [Gabriella Brown]

About the Exhibition

Welcome to "Fading Bytes: The Ephemeral Nature of Net Art," an online exhibition that explores the fragility and impermanence of our digital experiences through a curated selection of net art works. Inspired by the mission of Gallery 404 - The Museum of Broken net.art Art, this exhibition stands as a poignant reminder that even the most innovative and captivating digital artworks are not immune to the ravages of technological progress.

Featured Net Art Works

Who is Flora?

by Travis Alber

This net art work was inspired by the discovery of abandoned postcards chronicling the travels of a mysterious woman across 1940s America. The artist sought to breathe new life into these forgotten fragments of the past by translating them into an interactive digital narrative. However, due to the progress of the internet, the work's interactive elements and storytelling have now been lost to the digital ether.

Brandon

(1998-1999) by Shu Lea Cheang

"Brandon" is a net art work created by Taiwanese-American artist Shu Lea Cheang. The piece explores themes of identity, sexuality, and the blurring of boundaries between the online and offline worlds. The work is based on the true story of Brandon Teena, a transgender man who was murdered in 1993. Cheang's "Brandon" project used the internet as a platform to create an open-ended, collaborative narrative that evolved over time, inviting viewers to participate in the storytelling.

My Boyfriend Came Back from the War

by Olia Lialina (1996)

An early and influential work of net art, "My Boyfriend Came Back from the War" explores themes of isolation, communication, and the fragmentation of human relationships in the digital age. The piece uses the unique capabilities of the web, such as nested frames and hyperlinks, to create a nonlinear, fragmented narrative that mirrors the isolation and communication challenges of the characters.
